Rajasthan

The Magical Allure of the Desert State of India

A splash of colors, culture, and valor- Rajasthan is the land of glorious legends and folklores. Its spectacular cities painted in shades of pink, blue, and white, magnificent forts & fairytale palaces, golden desert dunes with long camel trains, colorful sarees & turbans, and ferocious tigers beckon you for an exciting adventure in the land of the Maharajas.

Join us as we take you on a royal expedition where marvelous architecture, dazzling culture, vibrant festivals, and warmhearted people bring alive the romance of the desert.

Rajasthan Special

Forts & Palaces

High rampart and magnificent facades of Rajasthani forts enclose elegant palaces and gardens within their confines. Glittering mirror-work, ornate sculptures, and exquisite stonework portray the opulence of the Rajputs. Amer Fort and Mehrangarh Fort are the two stunning forts while Umaid Palace and Hawa Mahal illustrate the royal chic.

Pilgrimages

Rajasthan’s beautiful temples and shrines embody the quiet spirituality of Rajasthan. Ajmer, the tomb of the Sufi Saint Khwaja Muin-ud-din Chishti, and Pushkar, a sacred Hindu pilgrimage, are the two most visited holy sites in Rajasthan.

Wildlife

The erstwhile hunting grounds of the Rajputs, now converted into national parks, promise you a thrilling experience. Spot the royal Bengal tiger on a tiger safari in the wilderness of Ranthambore or watch fuchsia flamingoes & elusive Siberian cranes frolicking in the swamps of Bharatpur.

Desert Safari

The golden dunes of Thar invite you for a thrilling desert safari. Ride on camelbacks like desert nomads, camp under the stars, watch a cultural extravaganza and taste the delectable local cuisine of Rajasthan during a safari on the dunes of Thar.

Fairs and Festivals

Rajsathan’s lively fairs & festival breathe life in the quiet desert landscape. Holi celebrations in Jaipur, Desert Festival in Jaisalmer, and Pushkar Camel Fair are some of the most spectacular festivals of Rajasthan.

Alluring Destinations of Rajasthan

Jaipur

This opulent pink city is the capital of Rajasthan and one of the three vortexes of the alluring Golden Triangle in India. Resplendent with stunning forts, charming palaces, luxurious heritage properties, and bustling bazaar; Jaipur is one of the illustrious destinations of India.

Udaipur

Painted serene in dappling white & gold, the lake city of Udaipur is set in a romantic milieu. Fringed by Aravallis with magnificent palaces built upon glistening lakes, Udaipur is a dream destination.

Read More Enquire Now Tour Packages

Jodhpur

A gorgeous oasis in blue, Jodhpur epitomizes the vibrant mosaic of Rajputana architecture and culture. The dramatic fortifications of the hilltop Mehrangarh Fort, the elegant charm of Umaid Bhavan Palace, and the tantalizing sights & sounds of the old city are the magical allure of Jodhpur.

Read More Enquire Now Tour Packages

Jaisalmer

Set on the edge of the Thar Desert, with long camel trains, magnificent havelis, and a huge fort dominating its landscape, Jaisalmer evokes visions of Arabian Night tales. A walk into the living fort of Jaisalmer takes you back into the bygone era.

Mount Abu

A lush retreat in the Aravalli Hills, Mount Abu is the only hill station in the desert state of Rajasthan. Picturesque landscapes, salubrious climate and, beautiful architecture mark this emerald oasis in the burning desert.

Ajmer

Ajmer is one of the arcane cities of Rajasthan widely known for the revered Ajmer Sharif Dargah. The city lies at the centre of the state majestically ringed by Ana Sagar Lake and the Aravalli hills.

Bikaner

Bikaner is one of the magical cities of Rajasthan packed with lots of historical jewels. Every corner of the city echoes the history and then be it the ornamental temples or the grandeur of forts.

Bharatpur

Bharatpur is the place for India’s most spectacular display of wildlife with a dash of awe-inspiring historical remnants. Around 178 km distance from the Rajasthan's capital, Jaipur will bring you to this dusty city. With history having its traces in 5th century BC, the city was nurtured by Matsya dynasty followed by Maharaja Suraj Mal.

Ranthambore

Ranthambore is mainly known for Ranthambore National Park, one of the most enthralling wildlife areas in India to spot the famous Bengal Tigers. It is named after the historic Ranthambore Fort which makes the place more enchanting.
